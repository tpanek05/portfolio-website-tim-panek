An Overview

The Problem

  • Students need a way to connect to each other for extra help, collaboration, and peer support
  • Students lead busy lives, making it difficult to study effectively and get good grades.
  • Students need to be successful in their classes to gain marketable skills and a desirable job.

The Objective

  • Provide students with a way to search for and connect with other students who have similar interests and who are engaged in similar areas of study.
  • Provide students with an efficient way to message other students by sending texts, photos, videos, and other files.
  • Provide students with a way to post articles, videos, images, and files to a newsfeed so they may receive assistance and feedback.

Low Fidelity User Testing

About The Tests

With informed consent and a test script, I conducted 3 user tests.

  • 1 man and 2 women were tested.
  • Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, all 3 participated in moderated remote tests.

Tasks for participants included:

  • Create an account.
  • Search for students, filter your results, view a student profile, and connect to a student.
  • Message a student.
  • Make a post for your "friends" to see and comment on.

Results

To make sense of my results, I used Jakob Nielsen's severity rating scale for usability problems.

"I'd like to be able to see students' interests and specializations to get to know them before I message them." - Participant"

"I'd like to be able to share a post that I see so others can see it too." - Participant"

From Low Fidelity to High Fidelity

Mood Board

By developing a mood board, I was able to help generate a creative direction for my high fidelity wireframes and mockups.

  • Demonstrates friendly, welcoming, reassuring feel that was outlined in the project brief.
  • Depicts a key message that a network of students can help maintain motivation needed for student success.
  • Illustrates that students can gain an enhanced perspective by sharing and reviewing resources and assignments.

User Flows

  1. Create a profile.
  2. Search for and filter your results so you can connect to a student who specializes in Math.
  3. Message another student.
  4. Create a post for other students to see, review, and comment on.
  5. Check to see if there are alerts so you don't miss anything important.
  6. View your profile so you can check comments/peer feedback.
